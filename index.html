Telehealth-Specific Rules

State-specific telehealth registration requirements and important nuances for Fountain's virtual care model.

⚠️ Telehealth laws are evolving rapidly. Verify current requirements before expanding into any state.

Key Principle

In most states, the location of the patient determines where the provider must be licensed — not the location of the provider. Fountain providers must hold an active license in each state where they see patients via telehealth.

State-by-State Telehealth Notes

StateTelehealth RegistrationKey Rule
CaliforniaFull license requiredCA treats telehealth = in-person. CA-licensed NP treating CA patients via telehealth must hold CA license. 103 NP Application rules apply.
TexasRegistration requiredOut-of-state providers treating TX patients must register with the TX Medical Board's Telehealth program. TMB rules apply to all telehealth encounters.
FloridaLicense onlyNo separate telehealth registration. Full FL license required. Autonomous practice since SB 1058 (2023).
New YorkLicense onlyFull NY license required. No separate telehealth permit. NY has strict prescribing rules — verify DEA compliance.
GeorgiaLicense onlyNo separate telehealth registration. GA license + CPA required for NPs.
ArizonaLicense onlyFull practice authority. No separate TH registration. eNLC compact member.
ColoradoLicense onlyCompact member. RX Authority required for NPs to prescribe.
PennsylvaniaRX license separatePA requires a separate prescriptive authority (PA-RX) registration. CPA required.
MichiganCPA watchFull practice authority proposed but not yet enacted (as of 2026). CPA still required.
IllinoisCSR + PMPCSR required before DEA can prescribe CS in IL. PMP registration required.

COVID-19 Telehealth Flexibilities — Status

Most pandemic-era telehealth waivers have expired. Do not rely on any temporary DEA or state waiver unless verified as still active.

For full state steps including telehealth-specific registrations, see the State Rules Table (PMP, CPA, CSR columns).

Common abbreviations and terms used in Fountain Vitality licensing and compliance work.

TermFull NameDefinition
AANPAmerican Association of Nurse PractitionersOne of two NP certifying bodies. Offers FNP-C certification.
ANCCAmerican Nurses Credentialing CenterCertifying body offering FNP-BC, PMHNP-BC and other NP certifications.
APRNAdvanced Practice Registered NurseUmbrella term for NPs, CRNAs, CNMs, and CNSs. Used interchangeably with NP in most Fountain contexts.

DNPDoctor of Nursing PracticeTerminal practice degree for NPs. Licensed and practice the same as NPs with MSN.
DODoctor of Osteopathic MedicinePhysician credential. Licensed through osteopathic medical boards (separate from MD boards in some states).
eNLCEnhanced Nurse Licensure CompactMulti-state RN license compact. 40+ member states. One license valid in all member states.
FCVSFederation Credentials Verification ServicePrimary source verification service for physician credentials. Used in MD/DO licensing.
FNPFamily Nurse PractitionerMost common NP specialty at Fountain. Certified as FNP-C (AANP) or FNP-BC (ANCC).
GLPGLP-1Glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists. Weight management medications (semaglutide, tirzepatide) offered by Fountain.
HRTHormone Replacement TherapyFountain service line for women.

MDDoctor of MedicinePhysician credential. Licensed through state medical boards.
MSLMulti-State LicenseOften noted in provider sheets to indicate a compact (eNLC) RN license valid in multiple states.
NFPNFP InsuranceFountain's malpractice insurance broker. Contact: Dave Wood / Nicole Conley.
NPNurse PractitionerAdvanced practice registered nurse with prescriptive authority. Fountain's primary provider type.
NPINational Provider Identifier10-digit public identifier for all healthcare providers. Type 1 = individual, Type 2 = organization.
NURSYSNurse License Verification SystemNCSBN database for verifying RN/LPN licenses and checking compact status.

PDMPPrescription Drug Monitoring ProgramSee PMP.
PMHNPPsychiatric-Mental Health NPNP specialty. Certified as PMHNP-BC (ANCC).
PMPPrescription Monitoring ProgramState database tracking controlled substance prescriptions. Providers must register before prescribing CS in most states.
