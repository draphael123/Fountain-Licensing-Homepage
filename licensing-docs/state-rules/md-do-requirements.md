# 🩺 MD/DO Licensing Requirements

## IMLC Compact

{% hint style="success" %}
**IMLC eligible physicians** can obtain expedited licensure in member states — typically 2–4 weeks vs. 8–16 weeks for standard applications. Apply via [imlcc.org](https://imlcc.org/).
{% endhint %}

**IMLC eligibility requires:**
- Full, unrestricted license in a principal state of licensure
- No disciplinary actions, criminal convictions, or controlled substance violations
- Board certification or 3+ years of postgraduate training

**Current IMLC member states:**
AL · AZ · CO · DE · GA · ID · IA · IL · IN · KS · KY · ME · MD · MI · MN · MS · MT · NE · NV · NH · NJ · NM · ND · OH · OK · OR · PA · SD · TN · TX · UT · VT · WA · WV · WI · WY · DC · Guam

## Standard Application Process

1. Verify medical school credentials via [FCVS](https://www.fsmb.org/fcvs/)
2. Submit state medical board application + fee
3. Provide proof of malpractice insurance (NFP COI)
4. Complete state-specific CME requirements
5. Obtain DEA registration per state — see [DEA SOP](../sops/dea-registration.md)
6. Apply for state CSR if required — see [CSR SOP](../sops/csr-application.md)
7. Register with state PMP
